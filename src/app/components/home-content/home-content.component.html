See how the envelope model works

The first step is simple: create envelopes, such as: car, home, health, education, shopping, supermarket, leisure, debt, emergency reserve and investments.

During the first month, emergency reserve and investment envelopes will not be used.

Enter how much you think you spend each month on each envelope. For example: car (R $ 300); house (R $ 1,000).

Throughout the month, add all expenses within the appropriate envelope.

At the end of the first month, compare the graphs in the report and check if the result was very different. Example: car (expected: R $ 300 / reality: R $ 600); house (estimated: R $ 1,000 / reality: R $ 1,100).

TIn the second month the challenge increases. As you already have a better idea of how much you spend, enter the amount corresponding to the expense in each envelope and distribute the money necessary to pay the expenses within each one.

The goal is to spend only the money that is inside the envelope.

Set aside 10% of net income for the Investments envelope and invest that money in an investment that is yielding more than inflation.

TWhat is left of the money (ideally 20% of net income) should be kept in the Emergency Reserve envelope. This amount must be invested in an investment that allows quick withdrawal of money.

Repeat this procedure each month until you can adjust the accounts to the ideal ratio.
